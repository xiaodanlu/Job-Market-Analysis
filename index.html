Introduction

The job market for data science and analytics has been booming in recent years, with companies in a variety of industries looking to use data-driven insights to make informed business decisions. At the same time, there are many students and job seekers who are looking for jobs every year. Therefore, an analysis of the hiring needs of this industry is necessary to provide insights to those preparing for a career in this field.

In this project, two datasets on job postings obtained through a web crawler exercise will be explored. This project aims to extract meaningful insights from the dataset through data visualization, such as current hiring locations and required skills for positions. Ultimately, the project hopes to provide a comprehensive understanding of the current data science and analytics job market and to keep job seekers and employers up-to-date on the latest trends and opportunities in this exciting field.

About the Data

In this project, we explore two datasets that represent the entire United States and the District of Columbia, respectively. Together, they contain approximately 850 job postings related to data science, analytics, and related fields, obtained through web crawling exercises and active as of April 14, 2023. The first step in performing data analysis is to clean these datasets. Therefore, I first selected the necessary information such as job title, company name, location, description, etc., and cleaned up the two datasets separately. Besides these two datasets, I got a third one by merging these two. More details about the datasets are presented in the "Methods" section. The next step is to further analyze the data by visualizing three different datasets.

USA Dataset

Data science and analytics-related job data across the United States

DC Dataset

The job data primarily pertains to DC but is not limited to the region and encompasses various data science and analytics-related positions.

Combined Dataset

The dataset after merging USA data and DC data.

Brief View of Data

U.S. Job Distribution

Before we delve into the specific job postings, it is essential to get a general idea of the distribution of active data science-related job openings across the United States as of April 14th, 2023. By understanding the current state of employment in data science and other related careers, we can gain valuable insights into the job market trends and identify areas of opportunity for both job seekers and employers.

Figure 1: The symbol map is made by Tableau that visualizes the distribution of hiring for data science and related careers across the United States. The size of the dots on the map represents the number of job openings in a given location, while the colors are used to represent different regions. Based on the map, it can be seen that companies in California, New York, and Washington have a higher number of job openings compared to other regions in the US.

To gain insights into the overall job market for data science-related jobs, we used the Tableau Symbolic Map, a powerful visualization tool that helps to highlight geographic trends in hiring. This visualization allows us to easily see the distribution of job openings across the US, with the size of the dots representing the number of available jobs and the colors representing the different regions. Understanding general trends in the data science job market can provide us with valuable insights that will inform our analysis of specific job postings later in the project. By identifying regions with the most job openings, we can determine which areas of the country may have the most competitive job markets and which regions may offer job seekers the best opportunities. Analyzing Figure 1, we can see that California and New York have the most job openings overall, indicating that these regions are likely the most fertile ground for job seekers in data science. Thus, job seekers can focus on these regions to increase their chances of finding the best job opportunities.

Analysis --- View of USA

Which companies are hiring?

Now that we have a general understanding of which regions in the United States have the most openings for data science-related careers, the next step is to identify the specific companies that are currently hiring. Analyzing the company names in a clean data set of all job postings can help us identify the top companies with the most job openings. By doing so, job seekers can focus their job search on companies that are actively hiring for data science positions, increasing their chances of finding relevant job opportunities.

Figure 2: The treemap visualization, created using Tableau, shows the companies with job openings for data science-related positions in the United States as of 04/14/2023. The size of each rectangle represents the number of job postings available at each company, with Upwork having the highest number of job openings among all companies.

To analyze and identify the specific companies that are currently hiring, we utilized the treemap visualization in Tableau. This visualization tool allows us to compare the number of job openings at different companies by displaying the size of each rectangle proportionally to the number of job postings. By examining the treemap, we can easily see which companies have the most data science job openings, and job seekers can focus their job search on these companies. As depicted in Figure 2, Upwork has the most job postings, far ahead of other companies. However, it's important to note that many other companies are also actively recruiting for data science-related positions and offer a wide range of job openings. Therefore, job seekers should not limit themselves to just one or two companies but should rather explore multiple job opportunities at different companies to increase their chances of finding a suitable job.

    

Where can we find jobs?

Knowing which companies are hiring for data science positions is only part of the job search process. Job seekers also need to know where to find these job postings and how to apply. Fortunately, there are many resources available for job seekers to find data science jobs posted online.

Figure 3: Packaged bubble plot visualization, made by Tableau, of top online job posting sites for data science-related positions in the US, as of 04/14/2023. The size of each bubble represents the number of job postings on each site, with Upwork, LinkedIn, and ZipRecruiter having the most job postings among all sites.

To investigate which sites have more online resources for data science job postings, we utilized Tableau's packaged bubble plot visualization. This tool enables us to compare the number of available jobs on different sites by displaying the size of each bubble. From the visualization, we can easily see that Upwork, LinkedIn, and ZipRecruiter have a high number of job postings. Job seekers can utilize these sites to access a plethora of job information and apply for job openings.

    

What is the Salary for Data Science Jobs?

Figure 4: Box plot graph comparing salary ranges for different types of data science jobs. The plot highlights the differences in salaries between data analyst positions and jobs related to machine learning, neural networks, deep learning, reinforcement learning, and natural language processing.

Many job seekers who are considering a career in data science often wonder about the expected salary for this profession. To answer this question, we analyzed salary data from various job postings and created a box plot graph using Plotly (Figure 4). This graph allowed us to compare salary average ranges for different types of data science jobs and identify any differences in salaries between positions.

After analyzing the box plot graph, we observed that data analyst positions typically have lower salaries compared to other similar roles. This is likely because data analyst positions are often entry-level roles that require less experience and expertise than other data science jobs. Conversely, jobs related to machine learning, deep learning, reinforcement learning, neural networks, and natural language processing tend to have higher average salaries than other data science jobs. This is because these roles require more specialized skills and experience in machine learning and artificial intelligence.

Furthermore, the box plot graph shows that there is a wide range of salaries for each type of data science job. This can be attributed to various factors, such as years of experience, specific skills, and industry. Therefore, job seekers should conduct thorough research on job opportunities and salaries within their field of interest. Overall, this analysis and visualization of salary data can be a valuable resource for job seekers considering a career in data science. By understanding the salary ranges for different types of data science jobs, job seekers can better evaluate job opportunities and negotiate salaries to secure a more fulfilling and financially rewarding career.

Analysis --- View of DC

Is Washington D.C. a good place for data science-related jobs
in terms of salary?

The analysis conducted so far has taken a US-wide perspective to understand the companies hiring for data science-related jobs, the websites they use to post jobs, and the salaries and requirements for different job types. However, as a student at Georgetown University, I have learned that most students in DSAN program are interested in jobs in the Washington, DC area. Therefore, the next step is focusing on analyzing the DC opening jobs. Currently, Figure 1 shows that there are many data science-related job openings in the DC area. Therefore, it is necessary to explore whether there is a significant difference in salary between these jobs in DC and other parts of the United States.

Figure 6: The box plot shows the comparison of average salaries for different job types in the District of Columbia and surrounding areas (Maryland and Virginia) and the rest of the United States. Salaries for data analyst, natural language processing, and blockchain jobs are significantly higher in the District of Columbia, while neural network job types have higher averages in other parts of the U.S.

To answer this question, I created a box plot based on the average salary for each job opening, broken down by job type. By comparing the average salary for each job category in the District of Columbia and its surrounding areas (including Maryland and Virginia) and the rest of the United States, we can conclude that salaries for certain job types do not differ significantly between regions. These job types include machine learning, reinforcement learning, deep learning, big data, and data science.

However, jobs such as data analyst, natural language processing, and blockchain developer have much higher averages in the District of Columbia. Interestingly, the neural networks job type has a higher average salary in other parts of the U.S. compared to the District of Columbia. The available data may not be entirely accurate due to limited data availability, but the box plots provide valuable insight into the salary ranges for various job types in Washington, D.C., and the rest of the United States. Overall, based on the available data, DC appears to be a good place for data science-related jobs, especially for data analysts, natural language processing specialists, and blockchain developers.

    

Conclusion

In summary, the project aimed to use visualizations to analyze the data science job market in the U.S. and provide insights into where there is more demand for hiring across the country, as well as job requirements and salaries for various job types. The analysis also delved into the Washington, D.C. job market, showing that the area is a good place to work in data science-related jobs, especially for data analysts, natural language processing specialists, and blockchain developers.

According to the analysis, California and New York currently have the highest number of job openings, and companies such as Upwork and many others are actively recruiting for data science-related positions. Job seekers can access a large number of job listings and apply for openings using online job posting sites such as Upwork, LinkedIn, and ZipRecruiter. It is important to note that job seekers should not limit themselves to one or two companies or job boards, but rather explore multiple job opportunities at different companies and platforms to increase their chances of finding the right job.

Regarding salary, data science jobs related to machine learning, deep learning, reinforcement learning, neural networks, and natural language processing tend to have higher average salaries than other data science jobs. Job seekers desiring higher-paying positions should consider focusing on these fields and gaining the skills and experience needed.

Overall, job seekers should focus on building their skills and experience, networking with professionals in the field, and using online resources to find job opportunities that match their career goals. By keeping up with industry trends and proactively searching for jobs, job seekers can increase their chances of finding suitable and rewarding work in the data science field.
